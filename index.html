02 — Problem & context

Tea is often consumed without attention.

With so many different teas available, people lose track of what they like, what they don’t, and what they want to try next.

Solution: ATEATUDE brings the focus back to tea.

A calm, distraction-free app that supports mindful tea moments, not social performance.

Who

People who see tea as a calm, intentional moment and want to keep track of what they enjoy, without social pressure.

Why now

We live in a fast, always-on world, while the need for slow, calm rituals keeps growing.

03 — Concept

ATEATUDE is a calm tea journal.

The app turns drinking tea into an intentional ritual instead of a background habit. Users can log the tea they drink, discover new teas, and save them for later — all in a quiet, distraction-free environment.

Target audience

Tea enjoyers of any age. People who see tea as a calm, intentional moment and want to keep track of what they enjoy, without social pressure.

Value proposition

ATEATUDE offers a calm, distraction-free way to enjoy and remember tea.

Ritual-first Taste notes Mood + reflection Minimal UI

04 — Research (short)

What I researched and what it changed

Desk research + inspiration + quick competitor scan.

Research types
  • Desk research on digital overstimulation & cognitive load
  • Research into Calm Technology and attention (Norman Group, Amber Case)
  • Inspiration research around tea culture, rituals & health
  • Competitor analysis of existing apps  (Untappd, MyTeaPal)
Top insights
  • Most apps prioritize engagement and stimulation, increasing cognitive overload.
  • Tea apps focus on social sharing and activity, not on the ritual itself.
  • There is no calm tool for personal tea logging and reflection.
Translation to concept

ATEATUDE focuses on one thing: logging tea.

  • No social pressure.
  • No unnecessary features.
  • Just a quiet place to log and remember tea.

08 — Design choices

10 — Process & reflection

What I learned

Design and development, step by step.

What went well

  • Translating the design into code smoothly
  • Setting up the app step by step without chaos
  • Building steady progress from the start

What was difficult

  • Keeping the UI simple while the codebase became complex
  • Connecting frontend and backend cleanly
  • Resisting the urge to keep adding features

What I learned

  • Small steps beat big rewrites
  • Stability comes from a clear structure and consistency
  • Simplicity is a decision you have to protect
